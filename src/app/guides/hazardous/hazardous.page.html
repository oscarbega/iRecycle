Hazardous Waste

Introduction

Welcome to our recycling guide on hazardous waste! Hazardous waste poses risks to human health and the environment if not handled properly. Let's explore how you can manage hazardous waste safely and responsibly.

What is Hazardous Waste?

Hazardous waste includes materials that are toxic, flammable, corrosive, or reactive. Common examples of hazardous waste include batteries, electronic waste (e-waste), solvents, pesticides, and certain household cleaners.

How to Manage Hazardous Waste

Follow these steps to manage hazardous waste safely:

  1. Identify hazardous waste materials in your home or workplace.
  2. Store hazardous waste in appropriate containers that are labeled and sealed tightly to prevent leaks and spills.
  3. Avoid mixing different types of hazardous waste as this can lead to chemical reactions and increased hazards.
  4. Research local hazardous waste disposal options, such as designated collection sites, drop-off events, or hazardous waste facilities.
  5. Transport hazardous waste to disposal facilities following safety guidelines and regulations.
  6. Never dispose of hazardous waste in regular trash bins, sinks, or storm drains.

Examples of Hazardous Waste

Common types of hazardous waste include:

  • Batteries (e.g., lead-acid batteries, lithium-ion batteries)
  • Electronic waste (e.g., computers, TVs, cell phones)
  • Paints and solvents
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Cleaning products (e.g., bleach, drain cleaners)
  • Mercury-containing devices (e.g., thermometers, fluorescent bulbs)
