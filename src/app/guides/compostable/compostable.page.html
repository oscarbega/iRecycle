Compostable Waste

Introduction

Welcome to our recycling guide on compostable waste! Composting is a natural process that transforms organic waste into nutrient-rich soil amendment. Let's explore how you can compost effectively.

Why Compost?

Composting helps divert organic waste from landfills, reduces methane emissions, and creates valuable compost for soil enrichment. By composting, you can contribute to a healthier environment and sustainable soil management.

How to Compost

Follow these steps to compost effectively:

  1. Collect organic waste, such as fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, and yard waste, in a compost bin or pile.
  2. Layer green materials (nitrogen-rich) and brown materials (carbon-rich) to create a balanced compost pile.
  3. Aerate the compost regularly by turning it with a pitchfork or compost aerator to promote decomposition.
  4. Monitor the moisture levels of the compost pile and adjust as needed to maintain a damp but not waterlogged environment.
  5. Once the compost is dark, crumbly, and earthy-smelling, it's ready to use in gardens, lawns, and planters.

Examples of Compostable Materials

Common types of compostable materials include:

  • Fruit and vegetable scraps
  • Coffee grounds and filters
  • Eggshells
  • Grass clippings and leaves
  • Wood chips and sawdust
  • Paper napkins and cardboard
