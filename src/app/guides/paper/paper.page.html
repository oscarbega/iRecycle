Paper and Cardboard

Introduction

Welcome to our recycling guide on paper and cardboard! Recycling these materials is crucial for environmental sustainability. Here's how you can do your part.

Why Recycle Paper and Cardboard?

Paper and cardboard recycling helps reduce landfill waste, conserves natural resources, and minimizes pollution. By recycling these materials, you can contribute to a cleaner and healthier environment.

How to Recycle Paper and Cardboard

Follow these steps to recycle paper and cardboard effectively:

  1. Collect all paper and cardboard waste in a designated bin or bag.
  2. Remove any non-recyclable contaminants, such as food residues or plastic coatings.
  3. Flatten cardboard boxes to save space and make transportation easier.
  4. Take your paper and cardboard to a recycling center or put them in your curbside recycling bin.
  5. Ensure that your recyclables are clean and dry to prevent contamination.

Examples of Recyclable Paper and Cardboard Items

  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Office paper and envelopes
  • Cardboard boxes and packaging
  • Cereal boxes and paper towel rolls
  • Cardboard egg cartons
